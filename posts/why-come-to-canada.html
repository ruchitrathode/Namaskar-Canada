Why Come to Canada as an Indian Student? Pros & Cons

The Dream Many Share

Canada has become a top destination for Indian students—but why? Imagine walking into your first class and hearing multiple accents, feeling the chill in the air, and knowing you’ve started a brand new chapter. For many of us, it’s not just about education, it’s about a better future, exposure, and a lifestyle upgrade.

If you’re coming here only because your friend is, take a pause. Think about your long-term goals.

Pros: What Makes Canada Worth It?

     
  • 🎓 Quality Education: Canadian universities like UofT, UBC, and McGill are globally respected.
  • 🧳 Pathway to PR: Study → Work Permit → PR is more straightforward compared to the US or UK.
  • 🌍 Cultural Diversity: You’ll find friends from Punjab, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, even Nigeria and the Philippines!
  • 💼 Work Opportunities: 20 hours/week part-time jobs + 3 years PGWP = strong ROI on your tuition.
Canada’s healthcare system is free (for PRs and sometimes students). This can save you thousands!

Cons: The Side You Shouldn't Ignore

  • ❄️ Extreme Winters: Be ready to face -20°C with snow storms. Invest in good boots and jackets.
  • 🏡 High Living Costs: Rent, groceries, and transportation can cost CAD 800–1500/month.
  • 🧠 Mental Pressure: Balancing part-time work, studies, and home sickness is not easy.
  • Racism Exists: Rare but real. Prepare mentally and stay strong. “Sab theek ho jaayega.”
Don’t blindly join any college. Research DLI number, job opportunities, location, and reviews.

🎯 Who Should Actually Come?

Not everyone needs to come to Canada. You should consider it if:

  • You want global exposure and education
  • You’re financially stable or have a loan plan in place
  • You’re okay with working hard and sacrificing comfort for a few years
Do a 6-month self-assessment. Live on a strict budget in India, work part-time, and manage studies before deciding.

📌 Things Nobody Tells You

  • Many part-time jobs are minimum wage and physically demanding (e.g., warehouse or Tim Hortons).
  • You may have to live with 5–6 roommates to save on rent.
  • Success doesn’t happen in 2 months—expect 2–3 years of hustle.
Your soft skills—like communication, attitude, and discipline—matter *a lot* in Canada.

📝 What I'd Tell My Younger Brother

If I had a younger brother asking me, I'd say: “Bhai, Canada is amazing, but only if you're coming with the right mindset. This is not a shortcut to a luxury life—it’s a long but rewarding path. You’ll learn independence, confidence, and global thinking here.”

Come to Canada not to run away from India, but to grow into a better version of yourself.
